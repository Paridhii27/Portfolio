Move A Bit

Move A Bit is a live motion capture project created during the Quantum Design Jam at The New School with IBM. The project brings the concepts of quantum computing to life through a fun and interactive experience. It visually showcases the concept of entanglement through the interaction of the viewer with the system. Just as a special correlation exists between qubits, the particles in Move a Bit have a special relationship with the subject in motion. Quibit is the basic unit in quantum computing into which quantum information is stored/ encoded. In this project, movement produces the quantum state with all quantum possibilities represented in different colors. Stillness represents the observed collapsed, classical state.

Exhibited at the Microscope Gallery for the Quantum Art Exhibition
