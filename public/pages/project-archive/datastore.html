Introduction

This project explores the concepts of data monopoly, data use transparency, memory, and digital identity. Taking the idea of browser cookies as a means of data collection, in this built world people have individual access to all their data and memories through an actual data Store baking these cookies. People can access memory bytes about their identity and experiences in life in the form of differently flavored cookies uniquely made for them.

Each cookie tells the person about really specific incidents of their life from the web making the person question how digitally vulnerable they can be and how twisted is the idea of data monopolisation.

Browser cookies are small text files that collect bits of data about users. Individually they cannot track who you are as a person, but all these small bits of data come together to form a digital identity which is riddled with biases of the technological system. These cookie flavours are representative of different emotions and memories that we have as individuals as interpreted by the digital system.

As the people delved more into the memories and what it means to them, they ultimately questioned how the Data store knew all this information about them. In this time of digital colonialism, data is becoming more and more of an integral part of our life. Pieces of technology people interact with on a daily basis collect all sorts of user data. This built world had utmost data transparency and brought up questions on who has access to our data? What data do they have access to and why?
